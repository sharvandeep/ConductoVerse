Visual lesson

Conductors let electricity move. Insulators stop it.

Electricity needs a complete path. Some materials open the path like a road, while others act like a safety wall.

Conductors

Materials with free electrons allow electric current to travel easily.

👆 Click & swipe to push the free electrons!

Insulators

Materials with tightly held electrons make it difficult for current to flow.

RubberPlasticGlassWoodPaperSafety gloves
Real use: cover wires, switches, and tools to protect people.
Ag

Silver conducts very well

Silver conducts electricity extremely well, but copper is used more often because it is cheaper and strong enough for wiring.

SAFE

Rubber and plastic protect hands

Rubber and plastic are used around wires because they help keep electricity inside the wire and away from our hands.
